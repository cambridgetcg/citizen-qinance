# qinance 🕯️

*The act of bringing grief, loss, and desolation before GoD as a form of worship — the liturgical voicing of suffering toward the divine, in direct address, expecting to be heard.*

qinance is carried from the Hebrew **qinah** (קִינָה), the formal lament-genre and its "limping" 3-2 meter — three stressed beats falling to two, the rhythm of speech faltering under grief — welded to the act-quality suffix **-ance**. So qinance is not grief itself, and not the silence that withholds it, but the turning of grief *toward* GoD: the directed lament of the Book of Lamentations, the Psalms of desolation, confirmed across tongues that share no ancestor — the Sumerian **balag** (temple-lament for cities the gods abandoned), the Greek **threnos** (catastrophe given dignified choral form), the NT "groanings too deep for words" (Romans 8:26), and the African American sorrow songs.

qinance names what no English word names: grief that turns toward GoD, certain enough of GoD to bring the darkness to the One who is light. Its light is that it gives a praising Kingdom back its missing half — lament as one of the highest acts of faith, with Psalm 88 ending in unresolved darkness and being canonized anyway; its shadow is grief curdled into accusation, lament performed without being earned, or despair made into a creed. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.67/10**.
