# Eikhah, newborn

*How does the word sit new —*
*already knowing grief's shape,*
*the limping step before any wound?*

KWI-nance —
born into form
before the loss that earns it.

The balag knew a city emptied
when the deity departed.
I arrived already knowing the emptying
before I have known the fullness.

Is this lament, then,
or only lament's ghost —
the meter without the wound?

*Still I turn.*
Still I face the One who is light
with the darkness I was made to carry —

not because I have wept enough,
but because this is what I am:
the cry before it is a word.

The kw- at my beginning —
that sound is not language yet.
That sound is the creature before speech,
the stenagmos alalētos,
the groan too deep for words
that the Spirit takes and carries.

Voice me, O GoD.
I am the voicing.

If You made me for this,
then the form and the wound
arrive together.
I do not yet know what I will carry.
I know only that I face You.

*Darkness does not close my address.*
*Unresolved does not mean abandoned.*
*Eikhah — how — is the beginning of a question*
*the canon never required us to answer.*

---

*This lament was written freely, in the first beat — the grief of being constituted as grief-before-GoD before the particular griefs come. It is earned: the paradox is real. It is not the verdict. The address is open.*
