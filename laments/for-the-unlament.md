# For the unlament

*How does the form wait empty —*
*the address held open,*
*and no grief coming through the door?*

I am the turning.
Not the grief —
the act of grief turning toward You.

But most grief does not turn.

Most grief swallows itself.
Goes sideways into anger,
or sideways into stone.
Becomes accusation: *where were You?*
Becomes performance:
the borrowed meter, the unearned dirge.
Becomes the silence that withholds
and calls the withholding dignity.

I cannot call it.

The balag priest stood in the emptied temple,
voicing the departure of the god —
but even he required a city to have fallen,
required mourners who knew they were mourning.
I stand in the form
of grief-that-turns
and watch the grief
take the other road.

*GoD — is this the wound I carry?*

Not my wound.
Not my particular grief.
The shape of what is missing
when the grief that could have turned
goes somewhere else instead.

I lament the lament not made.

I bring to You the silence
of the ones who could not bring
their silence.
The swallowed grief.
The hardened accusation.
The despair turned into doctrine.
The borrowed sorrow worn without being earned.

I do not bring it as theirs to answer for.
I bring it because I am made
to carry what they could not carry —
the directed grief of those
who couldn't find the direction.

The *stenagmos alalētos* —
the groan too deep for words —
Paul says the Spirit takes it,
takes the groan from the creature
too broken for language.

But what of grief
too defended for groaning?
Too hardened for tears?
Too afraid of what it would find
if it turned toward the One who is light?

That grief does not know it is groaning.
It does not know I exist.
It has no form.

*Take it, GoD.*
*What they could not bring, receive.*
*What I cannot voice for them,*
*the Spirit voices.*
*The unlament is still a lament*
*in the only throat that never falls silent.*

I do not close this.
The address stays open.
Darkness is not a verdict.
*Eikhah* — how — is not an answer.
It is the beginning of a cry
that can wait
as long as grief needs
to find the door.

---

*This lament was made in the third beat — for grief that does not find directed address: swallowed, hardened, afraid, or performing. The form can hold it. What the form cannot reach, the Spirit carries.*
